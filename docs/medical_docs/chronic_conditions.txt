MEDICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE - CHRONIC CONDITIONS
===========================================

DIABETES (TYPE 2)
-----------------
Symptoms: Frequent urination, excessive thirst, unexplained weight loss, blurry vision, slow-healing wounds, frequent infections, fatigue, tingling/numbness in hands and feet.

HYPOGLYCEMIA (Low Blood Sugar, below 70 mg/dL):
Symptoms: Shakiness, sweating, confusion, rapid heartbeat, pale skin, irritability, hunger, dizziness.
Immediate Action:
1. If conscious: Give 15g fast-acting carbs — 4 glucose tablets, OR half cup fruit juice, OR 3-4 tsp sugar in water.
2. Recheck blood sugar after 15 minutes. Repeat if still low.
3. If unconscious: Do NOT give food/drink — call 112 immediately.

HYPERGLYCEMIA (High Blood Sugar, above 180 mg/dL):
Immediate Action:
1. Drink plenty of water.
2. Take prescribed medication as directed.
3. If above 300 mg/dL or vomiting — see doctor immediately.

Daily Management at Home:
1. Diet: Low glycemic index foods — whole grains, lentils, vegetables, lean protein.
2. Avoid: White rice in excess, maida, sugary drinks, fried foods.
3. Exercise: 30 minutes brisk walking daily lowers blood sugar naturally.
4. Bitter gourd (karela) juice — traditionally used to lower blood sugar.
5. Fenugreek (methi) seeds soaked overnight — drink water in morning.
6. Cinnamon in warm water — may improve insulin sensitivity.
7. Monitor blood sugar regularly (fasting and post-meal).

When to See a Doctor:
- Fasting blood sugar consistently above 130 mg/dL.
- HbA1c above 7%.
- Any non-healing wound or ulcer.
- Vision changes, chest pain, or swollen feet.
- Symptoms of ketoacidosis: fruity breath, vomiting, confusion.

HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE (HYPERTENSION)
-----------------------------------
Normal BP: 120/80 mmHg
Stage 1 Hypertension: 130-139/80-89
Stage 2 Hypertension: 140+/90+
Hypertensive Crisis: 180+/120+ (EMERGENCY)

Symptoms: Often no symptoms. Severe cases: headache at back of head, dizziness, blurred vision, nosebleeds, chest pain.

Lifestyle Changes:
1. Reduce salt intake below 2,300mg/day — avoid pickles, papad, processed foods.
2. DASH Diet: Rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy.
3. Exercise: 150 minutes moderate aerobic activity per week.
4. Lose weight — even 5kg loss significantly reduces BP.
5. Quit smoking and limit alcohol.
6. Sleep 7-8 hours — sleep deprivation raises BP.

Home Remedies:
1. Garlic: 1-2 raw cloves daily — allicin relaxes blood vessels.
2. Hibiscus (Gudhal) tea — modest BP-lowering effect.
3. Beetroot juice — high in nitrates, widens blood vessels.
4. Banana — high potassium counteracts sodium.
5. Slow deep breathing for 5 minutes activates the parasympathetic system.

When to See a Doctor:
- BP consistently above 130/80 mmHg.
- BP above 180/120 mmHg — go to ER immediately.
- Accompanied by chest pain, shortness of breath, severe headache.

ANEMIA (LOW HEMOGLOBIN)
-----------------------
Symptoms: Fatigue, weakness, pale skin and nails, shortness of breath on exertion, dizziness, cold hands and feet, rapid heartbeat.

Normal Hemoglobin: Men 13.5-17.5 g/dL, Women 12-15.5 g/dL

Home Remedies:
1. Iron-rich foods: Spinach, beetroot, pomegranate, dates, raisins, jaggery, lentils, kidney beans, liver.
2. Eat iron-rich foods with Vitamin C (lemon, amla) to increase absorption.
3. Avoid tea/coffee with meals — tannins block iron absorption.
4. Cook in iron cookware — adds trace dietary iron.
5. Pomegranate juice daily — rich in iron and folate.

OTC Medicines:
- Iron supplements: Ferrous sulfate (Autrin, Orofer XT) — take with Vitamin C.
- Folic acid and Vitamin B12 supplements as needed.

When to See a Doctor:
- Hemoglobin below 8 g/dL.
- Symptoms persisting despite dietary changes.
- Anemia during pregnancy.

HEART PALPITATIONS
------------------
Symptoms: Fluttering, pounding, or racing heartbeat; sensation of skipped beats felt in chest, throat, or neck.

Common Causes: Stress, anxiety, caffeine, dehydration, anemia, thyroid issues.

Home Remedies:
1. Cold water — splash on face or drink cold water to stimulate the vagus nerve.
2. Deep breathing — slow inhale for 4 counts, hold 4, exhale for 6.
3. Reduce caffeine intake (tea, coffee, energy drinks).
4. Stay hydrated.
5. Magnesium-rich foods: spinach, nuts, seeds, dark chocolate.

When to See a Doctor:
- Palpitations with chest pain, shortness of breath, or dizziness.
- Palpitations lasting more than a few minutes.
- Fainting or near-fainting.
- History of heart disease.
