MEDICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE - RESPIRATORY, MUSCULOSKELETAL & EMERGENCY SIGNS
========================================================================

SORE THROAT / PHARYNGITIS
--------------------------
Symptoms: Pain/scratchiness in throat, difficulty swallowing, swollen glands, hoarse voice, mild fever.

Home Remedies:
1. Warm saltwater gargle — 1/2 tsp salt in 8oz warm water, 3–4x daily.
2. Honey and lemon in warm water or tea.
3. Ginger-tulsi (basil) tea — anti-inflammatory.
4. Clove or mulethi (licorice root) tea.
5. Turmeric milk at bedtime.
6. Steam inhalation.
7. Stay hydrated, avoid cold drinks.
8. Rest your voice.

OTC Medicines:
- Throat lozenges: Strepsils, Betadine Gargle.
- Antiseptic gargles: Hexidine, Chlorhexidine mouthwash.
- Paracetamol or Ibuprofen for pain and fever.

When to See a Doctor:
- Severe throat pain making swallowing impossible.
- High fever (above 39°C) with throat pain.
- White patches or pus on tonsils (strep throat).
- Rash along with sore throat (scarlet fever).
- Symptoms lasting more than a week.

BODY PAIN / MUSCLE ACHES (MYALGIA)
------------------------------------
Symptoms: Dull aching in muscles, stiffness, tenderness on touch, fatigue.

Common Causes: Overexertion, flu/viral infection, dehydration, stress, poor posture.

Home Remedies:
1. Rest — avoid strenuous activity.
2. Warm compress or hot water bag on painful areas.
3. Epsom salt bath — magnesium sulfate reduces muscle tension.
4. Gentle stretching and light movement.
5. Massage with warm mustard oil or coconut oil.
6. Stay hydrated — dehydration worsens muscle cramps.
7. Banana or potassium-rich foods for muscle cramps.

OTC Medicines:
- Paracetamol (Crocin 500mg/650mg) — pain and fever.
- Ibuprofen (Brufen 400mg) — anti-inflammatory.
- Combiflam (Ibuprofen + Paracetamol) — stronger pain relief.
- Diclofenac gel (Voltaren, Voveran) — topical for localized pain.
- Moov spray or Iodex — for external muscle pain relief.

When to See a Doctor:
- Severe pain after an injury (possible fracture).
- Muscle weakness, not just pain.
- Pain with rash and fever (possible viral illness like dengue/chikungunya).
- Chest muscle pain (rule out cardiac issue).

BACK PAIN
---------
Symptoms: Dull/sharp pain in lower, mid, or upper back, stiffness, reduced range of motion.

Home Remedies:
1. Apply heat (hot water bag) for muscle spasm — 15–20 mins.
2. Ice pack in first 48 hours of acute injury — reduces inflammation.
3. Gentle stretching: child's pose, cat-cow stretch.
4. Maintain proper posture when sitting (lumbar support).
5. Sleep on a firm mattress, avoid sleeping on stomach.
6. Massage with warm sesame oil or mustard oil.
7. Avoid prolonged sitting — take breaks every 30 minutes.

OTC Medicines:
- Ibuprofen (Brufen 400mg) or Naproxen — inflammation.
- Diclofenac gel topical application.
- Muscle relaxants: Cyclobenzaprine (prescription usually required).

When to See a Doctor:
- Pain radiating down one or both legs (sciatica).
- Numbness or tingling in legs.
- Back pain after a fall or accident.
- Pain with fever and urinary symptoms (kidney infection).
- Severe pain that doesn't improve after 2 weeks.

CONJUNCTIVITIS (PINK EYE)
--------------------------
Symptoms: Red/pink eyes, watery or sticky discharge, itching, burning, swollen eyelids.

Home Remedies:
1. Cold or warm compress on closed eyes — relieves discomfort.
2. Clean discharge with clean cotton soaked in warm water (wipe outward).
3. Avoid touching/rubbing eyes.
4. Do NOT share towels, pillows, or eye drops.
5. Rose water drops — soothing (not a treatment, comfort only).
6. Wash hands frequently.

OTC Medicines:
- Lubricating eye drops (artificial tears): Refresh, Tears Natural.
- Antihistamine eye drops for allergic conjunctivitis: Ketotifen.
- (Antibiotic eye drops require prescription for bacterial conjunctivitis.)

When to See a Doctor:
- Eye pain (not just irritation).
- Sensitivity to light (photophobia).
- Blurred vision that doesn't clear with blinking.
- Symptoms worsening after 2–3 days.
- Newborn with eye discharge.

EMERGENCY WARNING SIGNS — ALWAYS SEE A DOCTOR IMMEDIATELY
----------------------------------------------------------
These symptoms require emergency medical attention. Do NOT attempt home treatment:

1. CHEST PAIN — especially with sweating, arm/jaw pain (possible heart attack).
2. DIFFICULTY BREATHING — severe shortness of breath at rest.
3. SUDDEN SEVERE HEADACHE — worst headache of life (possible aneurysm).
4. STROKE SIGNS — face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty (FAST test).
5. LOSS OF CONSCIOUSNESS — fainting, unresponsiveness.
6. SEVERE ALLERGIC REACTION — swelling of lips/tongue/throat, hives + breathing difficulty (anaphylaxis).
7. HIGH FEVER IN INFANTS — any fever in babies under 3 months.
8. SEIZURES — especially first-time seizure or prolonged seizure.
9. COUGHING OR VOMITING BLOOD.
10. SEVERE ABDOMINAL PAIN — sudden, intense, unrelenting.
11. DIABETIC EMERGENCY — very low or very high blood sugar with altered consciousness.
12. POISONING OR OVERDOSE — call poison control immediately.
