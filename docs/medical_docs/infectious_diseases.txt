DENGUE FEVER
------------
Symptoms: Sudden high fever (104°F/40°C), severe headache, pain behind eyes, joint and muscle pain ("breakbone fever"), skin rash (appears 2-5 days after fever), mild bleeding (nose, gums), fatigue, nausea, vomiting.

Severity Levels:
- Dengue Fever (mild): Manageable at home with monitoring.
- Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever: Requires hospitalization — bleeding, platelet drop.
- Dengue Shock Syndrome: Life-threatening emergency.

Home Remedies:
1. Papaya leaf juice — widely used in India; studies suggest it may help increase platelet count. Extract juice from 2 fresh leaves, drink 2 tbsp twice daily.
2. Stay extremely well hydrated — water, coconut water, ORS, fresh juices.
3. Giloy (Tinospora cordifolia) juice — traditional immune booster.
4. Neem leaf extract — antiviral properties.
5. Complete bed rest — body needs all energy for recovery.
6. Eat soft, easily digestible foods — khichdi, dal, rice, soup.
7. Use mosquito nets and repellents to prevent spreading to others.

OTC Medicines:
- Paracetamol (Crocin/Dolo 650) — ONLY safe painkiller. 500-650mg every 6 hours.
- NEVER take Ibuprofen, Aspirin, or Diclofenac — increases bleeding risk severely.
- ORS (Electral) — to maintain electrolyte balance.

When to See a Doctor IMMEDIATELY:
- Platelet count drops below 1,00,000 (normal: 1.5-4 lakh).
- Severe abdominal pain or tenderness.
- Persistent vomiting (3+ times in a day).
- Bleeding from nose, gums, or in urine/stool.
- Rapid breathing, cold/clammy skin.
- Restlessness or sudden drowsiness.

MALARIA
-------
Symptoms: Cyclical fever with chills (every 48-72 hours), shaking chills before fever, profuse sweating after fever breaks, headache, nausea, vomiting, muscle pain, fatigue. Symptoms appear 10-15 days after mosquito bite.

Home Remedies (supportive only — malaria REQUIRES medical treatment):
1. Stay well hydrated — fever causes heavy fluid loss.
2. Ginger and cinnamon tea — may help with nausea.
3. Cold compress during high fever.
4. Light, nutritious diet — fruits, soups, khichdi.
5. Mosquito nets at night — essential to prevent spreading.
6. Neem and tulsi leaves boiled in water for steam inhalation.

OTC Medicines:
- Paracetamol for fever management only.
- Malaria REQUIRES prescription antimalarial drugs (Chloroquine, Artemisinin combinations) — do NOT self-medicate.

When to See a Doctor:
- ANY suspected malaria should be confirmed with a blood test.
- Cerebral malaria signs: confusion, seizures, loss of consciousness.
- Severe anemia, jaundice, or difficulty breathing.
- Malaria in pregnant women is a medical emergency.

TYPHOID FEVER
-------------
Symptoms: Gradually increasing fever over 3-4 days (up to 104°F), headache, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, constipation or diarrhea, weakness, coated tongue.

Home Remedies:
1. Complete bed rest — essential for recovery.
2. High-calorie soft diet — bananas, boiled potatoes, rice, dal, curd.
3. Stay well hydrated — ORS, coconut water, fruit juices.
4. Cold compress for high fever.
5. Garlic — natural antimicrobial; 2-3 cloves daily.
6. Cloves boiled in water — drink as tea.
7. Avoid raw vegetables, spicy/fatty foods, alcohol.

OTC Medicines:
- Paracetamol for fever control.
- Typhoid requires antibiotics (Azithromycin, Ciprofloxacin) — PRESCRIPTION required.
- Do NOT self-prescribe antibiotics.

When to See a Doctor:
- Any suspected typhoid must be diagnosed with Widal test or blood culture.
- Intestinal perforation signs: sudden severe abdominal pain.
- Confusion, extreme weakness.
- Fever persisting beyond 5 days without improvement.

CHIKUNGUNYA
-----------
Symptoms: Sudden fever, severe joint pain (especially wrists, ankles, knees), muscle pain, headache, rash, fatigue. Joint pain can persist for months.

Home Remedies:
1. Complete rest — joint pain worsens with activity.
2. Apply warm compress to painful joints.
3. Turmeric milk — powerful anti-inflammatory.
4. Giloy juice — helps with fever and immunity.
5. Stay hydrated.
6. Gentle range-of-motion exercises once acute fever passes.

OTC Medicines:
- Paracetamol — for fever and mild pain.
- AVOID NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Aspirin) during acute phase.
- Chlorzoxazone (muscle relaxant) with doctor advice for joint pain.

When to See a Doctor:
- Joint pain lasting more than 1 week.
- Neurological symptoms: confusion, weakness.
- Severe rash or skin peeling.
- Elderly or immunocompromised patients should always see a doctor.
