MEDICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE - COMMON ILLNESSES, SYMPTOMS & TREATMENTS
================================================================

FEVER
-----
Symptoms: Body temperature above 38°C (100.4°F), chills, sweating, headache, muscle aches, dehydration, weakness.

Severity Levels:
- Mild (38–38.9°C): Usually manageable at home.
- Moderate (39–39.9°C): Monitor closely, consider OTC medication.
- High (40°C+): Seek medical attention.

Home Remedies:
1. Stay hydrated — drink plenty of water, coconut water, or ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution).
2. Rest in a cool, well-ventilated room.
3. Apply a cool, damp cloth to the forehead, wrists, and neck.
4. Wear light clothing and use a light blanket.
5. Drink herbal teas like tulsi (basil) or ginger tea with honey.
6. Lukewarm sponge bath to reduce temperature.

OTC Medicines:
- Paracetamol (Crocin, Dolo 650) — 500–650mg every 4–6 hours, do not exceed 4g/day.
- Ibuprofen (Brufen, Advil) — 400mg every 6–8 hours with food. Avoid on empty stomach.

When to See a Doctor:
- Fever above 103°F (39.4°C) lasting more than 2 days.
- Fever in infants under 3 months.
- Accompanied by stiff neck, rash, confusion, difficulty breathing.
- Febrile seizures occur.

COMMON COLD
-----------
Symptoms: Runny nose, nasal congestion, sneezing, sore throat, mild cough, mild fever, fatigue.

Home Remedies:
1. Steam inhalation — add eucalyptus oil or Vicks to hot water, inhale for 10 minutes.
2. Saltwater gargling — 1/2 tsp salt in warm water, gargle 3x daily.
3. Honey and ginger tea — boil ginger in water, add honey and lemon.
4. Turmeric milk (Haldi doodh) — anti-inflammatory and immunity boosting.
5. Chicken soup or warm broths help clear nasal passages.
6. Stay hydrated and rest well.
7. Use a humidifier in the room.
8. Nasal saline rinse (neti pot) to clear congestion.

OTC Medicines:
- Cetirizine (Cetzine, Alerid) — for runny nose/sneezing, 10mg once daily.
- Phenylephrine nasal drops (Nasivion) — for congestion relief.
- Vitamin C supplements (500mg daily) may reduce duration.

When to See a Doctor:
- Symptoms last more than 10 days.
- High fever, chest pain, or difficulty breathing.
- Symptoms worsen after initial improvement (possible bacterial infection).

COUGH
-----
Types:
- Dry cough: No mucus, tickling sensation in throat.
- Wet/Productive cough: With mucus/phlegm.

Home Remedies:
1. Honey — 1-2 tsp raw honey, especially at bedtime (do NOT give to children under 1 year).
2. Ginger tea with honey and black pepper.
3. Turmeric milk with a pinch of black pepper.
4. Steam inhalation helps loosen mucus in wet cough.
5. Gargle with warm salt water.
6. Stay hydrated — thins mucus.
7. Avoid cold drinks, ice cream, and dairy if cough worsens.
8. Elevate head while sleeping.

OTC Medicines:
- Dry cough: Dextromethorphan syrups (Benadryl Dr, Honitus).
- Wet cough: Expectorants like Guaifenesin (Ascoril, Mucinex).
- Throat lozenges: Strepsils, Halls for soothing irritation.

When to See a Doctor:
- Cough persists more than 3 weeks.
- Coughing up blood or dark-colored mucus.
- Chest pain, shortness of breath.
- Sudden weight loss with cough (rule out TB or other conditions).

HEADACHE
--------
Types: Tension headache, migraine, cluster headache, sinus headache.

Symptoms: Dull/throbbing pain, pressure around head, sensitivity to light/sound (migraine), nausea.

Home Remedies:
1. Drink water — dehydration is a major cause.
2. Cold or warm compress on forehead/neck.
3. Peppermint oil applied to temples (diluted with carrier oil).
4. Rest in a dark, quiet room.
5. Ginger tea for migraine-related nausea.
6. Massage the temples, neck, and shoulders.
7. Avoid screens and bright lights.
8. Regular sleep schedule prevents tension headaches.

OTC Medicines:
- Paracetamol (Crocin 500mg).
- Ibuprofen (Brufen 400mg) — for inflammation-related headaches.
- Aspirin 325mg (avoid in children under 16).
- Combination tablets: Combiflam (Ibuprofen + Paracetamol).

When to See a Doctor:
- Sudden, severe "thunderclap" headache (worst of your life).
- Headache with fever, stiff neck, confusion (meningitis signs).
- Headache after a head injury.
- Progressive worsening over days/weeks.
- Headache with vision changes, weakness, or speech difficulty.
