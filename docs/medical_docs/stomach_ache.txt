MEDICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE - DIGESTIVE & SKIN CONDITIONS
=====================================================

STOMACH ACHE / ABDOMINAL PAIN
------------------------------
Symptoms: Cramping, bloating, nausea, diarrhea, constipation, gas, indigestion.

Common Causes: Indigestion, gas, IBS, food poisoning, gastritis, constipation.

Home Remedies:
1. Ginger tea — natural anti-nausea, aids digestion.
2. Peppermint tea — relieves gas and bloating.
3. Warm compress or heating pad on the abdomen.
4. BRAT diet (Bananas, Rice, Applesauce, Toast) for stomach upset.
5. Ajwain (carom seeds) with warm water — excellent for gas and indigestion.
6. Jeera (cumin) water — boil cumin seeds in water, drink warm.
7. Avoid fatty, spicy, or fried foods.
8. Small, frequent meals instead of large ones.
9. Probiotics: Curd/yogurt helps restore gut flora.

OTC Medicines:
- Antacids: Gelusil, Digene — for acidity/indigestion.
- Simethicone (Gas-O-Fast) — for bloating and gas.
- Domperidone (Domstal) — for nausea and bloating.
- Oral Rehydration Solution (Electral) — if diarrhea accompanies.
- Loperamide (Imodium) — for acute diarrhea (not for bloody diarrhea).

When to See a Doctor:
- Severe, sudden abdominal pain.
- Pain with fever above 38.5°C.
- Vomiting blood or black stools.
- Pain that worsens significantly over 6 hours.
- Suspected appendicitis (pain shifts to lower right abdomen).

DIARRHEA
--------
Symptoms: Loose watery stools 3+ times/day, cramping, urgency, nausea.

Home Remedies:
1. ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution) — crucial to prevent dehydration.
2. Coconut water — natural electrolytes.
3. BRAT diet — gentle on the stomach.
4. Rice water (kanji) — boil rice and drink the starchy water.
5. Avoid dairy, fatty foods, caffeine, alcohol.
6. Psyllium husk (Isabgol) with water — helps firm stools.
7. Curd with rice — restores gut bacteria.

OTC Medicines:
- Electral / Enerzal — ORS for rehydration.
- Loperamide (Imodium 2mg) — slows gut motility.
- Probiotics: Econorm, Vibact — restore gut flora.

When to See a Doctor:
- Diarrhea lasting more than 2 days.
- Signs of severe dehydration (dry mouth, no urination, dizziness).
- Blood or mucus in stools.
- High fever with diarrhea.
- Diarrhea in infants or elderly.

ACIDITY / ACID REFLUX / GERD
-----------------------------
Symptoms: Heartburn, sour belching, burning sensation in chest, nausea after meals, bloating.

Home Remedies:
1. Cold milk or buttermilk — immediate relief from acidity.
2. Banana — natural antacid.
3. Coconut water — alkaline, soothes stomach lining.
4. Fennel seeds (saunf) after meals — aids digestion, reduces acid.
5. Amla (Indian gooseberry) juice in the morning.
6. Avoid spicy, oily, fried foods and carbonated drinks.
7. Eat smaller meals, don't lie down for 2 hours after eating.
8. Elevate head of bed by 6–8 inches.
9. Avoid tea/coffee on empty stomach.

OTC Medicines:
- Antacids: Gelusil MPS, Digene, Eno (for immediate relief).
- H2 Blockers: Ranitidine/Famotidine — reduce acid production.
- Omeprazole (Omez 20mg) — PPI, taken 30 min before meals.

When to See a Doctor:
- Heartburn more than 2x per week.
- Difficulty swallowing.
- Unintentional weight loss.
- Persistent vomiting.
- Chest pain (rule out cardiac cause first).

SKIN RASH / ALLERGIC REACTION
------------------------------
Symptoms: Redness, itching, hives, swelling, dry/flaky skin, bumps.

Home Remedies:
1. Aloe vera gel — cooling, anti-inflammatory, apply directly.
2. Cold compress — reduces itching and swelling.
3. Oatmeal bath — soothing for widespread rash.
4. Coconut oil — moisturizes and has antimicrobial properties.
5. Calamine lotion — drying agent for itchy rashes.
6. Avoid scratching (causes infection).
7. Identify and remove the trigger (food, detergent, plant, etc.).
8. Loose cotton clothing to reduce irritation.

OTC Medicines:
- Antihistamines: Cetirizine (10mg), Loratadine (10mg) — for allergic rash.
- Hydrocortisone cream (1%) — mild steroid for inflammation/itch.
- Calamine lotion — for heat rash or mild contact dermatitis.

When to See a Doctor:
- Rash spreads rapidly or covers large areas.
- Rash with fever, joint pain, or difficulty breathing (anaphylaxis emergency).
- Blistering rash or open sores.
- Rash near eyes, mouth, or genitals.
- No improvement after 3–5 days of home treatment.

MINOR CUTS AND WOUNDS
---------------------
Home Remedies:
1. Apply pressure with a clean cloth to stop bleeding.
2. Clean with clean running water for 5 minutes.
3. Apply turmeric paste — natural antiseptic.
4. Aloe vera gel speeds up healing.
5. Keep wound covered with a clean bandage.

OTC Medicines:
- Antiseptic: Betadine (Povidone-Iodine) solution or cream.
- Neosporin / Soframycin antibiotic ointment — prevents infection.
- Sterile bandages and adhesive plasters.

When to See a Doctor:
- Deep wound that won't stop bleeding after 10 minutes of pressure.
- Wound from a rusty/dirty object (tetanus risk).
- Signs of infection: redness spreading, pus, fever.
- Animal or human bite.
